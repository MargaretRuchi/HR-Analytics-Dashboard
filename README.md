# HR-Analytics-Dashboard
HR Analytics Dashboard built using Python, Pandas, Matplotlib, Seaborn, and Google Colab to analyze employee attrition, job satisfaction, overtime impact, and workforce trends.
HR Analytics Dashboard: Employee Attrition and Workforce Insights
#Project Overview

Employee retention is a critical challenge for organizations seeking to maintain productivity, reduce recruitment costs, and preserve institutional knowledge. This project analyzes workforce data to identify factors influencing employee attrition and uncover insights that can support data-driven HR decision-making.

Using Python and Google Colab, employee data was cleaned, explored, and visualized to examine workforce demographics, job satisfaction, overtime patterns, departmental trends, and employee turnover.

Business Problem

High employee turnover can negatively impact organizational performance and increase hiring and training costs.

This project aims to answer the following business questions:

What is the employee attrition rate?
Which departments experience the highest turnover?
Does overtime contribute to attrition?
How does job satisfaction affect employee retention?
Which employee groups are most likely to leave?

Key Findings
Employee Attrition Rate is 16.12%

The organization experiences moderate employee turnover, indicating opportunities for improving employee retention strategies.

Research & Development Has the Highest Number of Employee Exits

Research & Development recorded the largest number of attrition cases, followed by Sales.

Overtime is Strongly Associated with Attrition

Employees working overtime are significantly more likely to leave the organization than those who do not work overtime.

Younger Employees Have Higher Attrition Rates

Employees who leave tend to be younger than those who remain with the company.

Lower Job Satisfaction is Associated with Higher Turnover

Employees reporting lower job satisfaction levels are more likely to leave the organization.

Income Distribution Shows Salary Concentration

Most employees earn within the lower-to-middle income range, with fewer employees earning significantly higher salaries.

Business Recommendations
1. Reduce Excessive Overtime

Implement workforce planning and workload balancing initiatives to reduce employee burnout.

2. Improve Employee Retention Programs

Develop career growth pathways, mentorship programs, and employee engagement initiatives for younger employees.

3. Monitor Employee Satisfaction

Conduct regular employee satisfaction surveys and address concerns proactively.

4. Review Compensation Strategies

Evaluate salary competitiveness and benefits packages, particularly for critical roles.

5. Focus on High-Turnover Departments

Implement targeted retention initiatives within Research & Development and Sales departments.
